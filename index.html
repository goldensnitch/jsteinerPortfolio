Dr. Augustine Papali is a graduate of the Harvard Divinity School and an immigrant from South India. After accruing a breadth of experiences from his many years in the US, I helped bring his stories to a wider audience.

Telling the story

Dr. Papali contacted me in the summer of 2016 asking for help sharing his experiences with the world. He wanted a blog that allowed him to periodically post op-eds that he had written over the years, as well as interact with his readers in a forum-like manner. Given the requirement that he did not want to spend a dime on hosting data, but still maintain his own domain, this presented a challenge.

Blogging on a budget

In order to meet his needs, while not incurring any costs, I used Medium.com as a data store to host the actual blog posts and comments. The idea was that users would land on his website and see blog snippets, parsed from his account on Medium, and then be able to click on them to go to the actual article. Since Medium did not have any formal API for this, I employed Yahoo Query Language (YQL) to serve as an endpoint for scraping their website.

Difference.
Changing the world through passive donations

+ UX/UI Designs

+ Mockups

+ User Testing

A semester long project to design an application that rounds up user purchases to the nearest dollar and donates the difference to charities of their choice.

Make giving easy

Everyone has some organization whose mission they support. While donating $50-100 at one time can often be tough to stomach, people hardly notice when they toss a dime or quarter in the jar. With Difference we took this concept and ran with it, the idea being that we could aggregate these small deltas in user purchases over a month and allocate the sum to a pre-selected group of organizations they supported.

User-centric design

We designed the application with the goal of maximizing a user's inclination to keep up with their donations. In our research, we observed that users of similar apps felt most engaged when there was a social component. After running through multiple mockups and extensive user testing, we ended up with our final UI.
